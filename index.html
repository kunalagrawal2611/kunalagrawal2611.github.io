About

The researcher
behind the work

I am a Computer Science PhD candidate at the University of Dayton (GPA 3.96), working in the Vision and Mixed Reality Lab under Dr. Tam Nguyen. My research sits at the intersection of deep learning, generative AI, and human visual perception — connecting how machines see to how humans remember.

Before my PhD, I completed my Master's with a perfect 4.00 GPA and the Outstanding Graduate Student in Computer Science Award. I interned at Tan Tock Seng Hospital, Singapore, building clinical CV pipelines across 60,000+ medical images. I am AWS Certified and have shipped open-source tools in TypeScript and Kotlin.

I serve as Lead Instructor for Python lab sessions, peer-review submissions for international CV and ML conferences, and mentor junior graduate students through the publication pipeline. Outside the lab, I spent three years in leadership roles with Rotaract in India, serving as President and Joint Secretary.
